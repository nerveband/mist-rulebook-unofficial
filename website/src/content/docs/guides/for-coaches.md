Everything you need to know as a MIST team coach.

## Coach Requirements

- Must be **21 years of age or older**
- Can be a parent, teacher, administrator, sibling, or trusted adult
- Must be **registered through MyMIST**
- At least one coach must be **on campus at all times**
- If a coach must leave, they **must have a replacement**

## Registration Process

1. **Register through MyMIST** before the deadline
2. Ensure all team members are properly registered
3. Verify each student is registered for correct competitions
4. Confirm early submissions are uploaded on time
5. Review and accept responsibility for team conduct

## Your Responsibilities

### Before the Tournament
- [ ] Complete coach registration on MyMIST
- [ ] Verify all student registrations
- [ ] Ensure early submissions are uploaded (2 weeks prior)
- [ ] Communicate schedule and expectations to students
- [ ] Arrange transportation
- [ ] Plan for entire tournament duration (no early departures)

### During the Tournament
- [ ] Accompany team at all times
- [ ] Ensure students arrive on time for competitions
- [ ] Monitor student behavior and dress code compliance
- [ ] Be available for emergencies
- [ ] Know where your students are at all times
- [ ] Stay until the tournament officially ends

## Supervision Rules

### Attendance
- At least one registered coach must be present **at all times**
- Students may not leave the venue without a coach from their team
- If you must leave temporarily, ensure another registered coach is present

### Early Departure
Students wanting to leave early must:
1. Request permission from MIST **7+ days in advance**
2. Contact Regional Registration Headquarters
3. Once approved, notify you (the coach) before leaving

### Communication
- Monitor official MIST communications (email, app, social media)
- Relay important updates to your team
- Students are responsible for reading their own communications too

## Team Management Tips

### Preparing Your Team
1. **Hold pre-tournament meetings** - Review rules and expectations
2. **Create a schedule** - Help students know when/where to be
3. **Assign responsibilities** - Especially for group projects
4. **Practice run-throughs** - For presentations and performances
5. **Review dress code** - Professional and formal attire required

### Tournament Day Tips
1. **Arrive early** - Build in buffer time
2. **Have a meeting point** - Where to gather if separated
3. **Collect phone numbers** - For emergency contact
4. **Bring supplies** - Snacks, water, first aid basics
5. **Stay positive** - Your energy affects the team

## What Judges Need From You

- Ensure students arrive on time
- Do **not** communicate with competitors during rounds
- Remain silent during competitions
- Follow audience guidelines when spectating
- Model good sportsmanship

## Day-of Checklist

### Morning
- [ ] Confirm team attendance
- [ ] Distribute schedules
- [ ] Remind students of competition times
- [ ] Collect any hard copies needed for submissions
- [ ] Review dress code compliance

### Throughout the Day
- [ ] Track team members' locations
- [ ] Remind students of upcoming competitions
- [ ] Ensure workshop attendance (for spirit points)
- [ ] Handle any issues that arise
- [ ] Keep spirits high!

### End of Day
- [ ] Account for all team members
- [ ] Debrief with the team
- [ ] Confirm transportation arrangements
- [ ] Stay until official dismissal

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Student missing a competition | Contact MIST organizers immediately |
| Lost badge | See MIST Representative for replacement |
| Early departure needed | Must be requested 7+ days in advance |
| Competition time conflict | Contact Regional HQ before tournament |
| Student illness | Contact MIST organizers; may need documentation |

## Contact Information

Keep handy:
- Regional Headquarters contact info
- MIST organizers' contact info
- Emergency venue contacts
- School/parent emergency contacts for students
