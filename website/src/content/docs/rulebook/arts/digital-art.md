If you're the Photoshop or Illustrator whiz that everyone goes to for event flyers, the digital art competition is for you. Pixelate away!

**Format:** In-Person  
**Early Submission:** Two weeks prior to tournament

:::note[Changed in 2026]
Graphic Design has been renamed to Digital Art.
:::

## Early Submission Requirements
- Artwork (JPG, TIFF, or PNG format)
- Written artist statement (500 words maximum)
- A minimum of 2 drafts of artwork process

## Registration Guidelines

1. No more than **four (4) students** from each school can register for this competition.
2. This competition is **NOT** a team effort. Each competitor must turn in a separate piece of work.

## Procedures

1. All artwork must be labeled with **MIST ID ONLY**.
   - If competitor name appears anywhere on the artwork, points will be deducted.

2. Artwork must reflect on this year's theme.

3. Artwork should **avoid realistic, representational rendering of lifelike human or animal form**. If in doubt, contact your Regional Competitions Headquarters.

4. Students are **NOT** permitted to utilize AI Generators.
   - This includes but is not limited to Dall-E 2, MidJourney, Nightcafe, Deep Dream, and/or Stable Diffusion.
   - Usage of AI Generators is grounds for elimination from the competition.

5. Competitors should **ONLY use their computers** to generate any artwork for this competition.
   - Hand-created artwork will not be considered.

6. Artwork should **not be animated**.

7. A typed short explanation (500 words max.) must be included with the artwork explaining what it's about and what inspired you.

8. Judging will be based on the explanation provided in the interview and the artwork itself.

9. The interview will be no longer than **five (5) minutes**.

10. If more than **15 entries**, only top 15 (based on artwork and written statement) will be interviewed.
    - An open gallery preliminary judging round will take place.
    - Top 15 will have a 5-minute interview with the Judges' Panel.

## Checklist
- [ ] Digital Art submission (JPG, TIFF, or PNG)
- [ ] Minimum of 2 drafts of Digital Art process
- [ ] Written statement based on interview questions (500 words max)
- [ ] Both submission and written statement labeled with MIST ID and competition name

## Interview Questions

Judges will ask:
1. How did you aim to convey this year's theme in your work? In what ways does your artwork represent your individual point of view, imagination, creativity, and individuality?
2. Explain your purpose in developing this artwork and describe how you achieved that goal. Describe any influences from art history, artists, or art periods or styles that contributed to the development of this artwork.
3. What elements or principles of art did you use, and how did you select and organize the visual aspects of this work?
