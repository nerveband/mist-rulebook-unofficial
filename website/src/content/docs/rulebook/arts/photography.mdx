Showcase your photography skills with a portfolio that reflects this year's theme through a coherent visual narrative.

## Format
In-Person

## Submission Requirements

**On-Site:**
- Photography Portfolio (3-5 photographs)
- Written artist statement (500 words maximum)

**Early Submission:** None

## Registration Guidelines

1. No more than **four (4) students** from each school can register.
2. This competition is **NOT** a team effort. Each competitor must turn in separate work.

## Procedure

<Steps>

1. All artwork must be labeled with **MIST ID ONLY**.
   - Competitor name must not appear anywhere.

2. Submit **3-5 photographs** with a coherent theme or style to showcase your skill.

3. Pictures must reflect on this year's **theme** and can be of any inanimate OR animate object.

4. All work must be the competitor's own from beginning to end, including taking the pictures.

5. Pictures must be taken **exclusively for this competition** (no previous years).

6. Photographs may **NOT be digitally modified** beyond standard optimization:
   - Dust removal
   - Cropping
   - Minor exposure, color, and contrast adjustments

7. Each participant interviewed for **5 minutes** to describe purpose and inspiration.

8. If more than **15 entries**, only the top 15 (based on artwork and written statement) will be interviewed.
   - Open gallery preliminary judging round first.
   - Top 15 get 5-minute interview with Judges' Panel.

9. Include a written statement **(500 words max.)** explaining your artwork, inspiration, and answers to interview questions.

10. Competitors judged according to the Photography Competition Ballot.

</Steps>

<Aside type="note" title="Important">
The written statement is an integral component of qualifying for an interview in the top fifteen.
</Aside>

## Checklist

- [ ] Photography portfolio (3-5 photos with coherent theme)
- [ ] Written statement based on interview questions (Section D)
- [ ] Both submission and statement labeled with MIST ID and competition name

## Interview Questions

Judges will ask:

1. How did you aim to convey this year's theme in your work? In what ways does your artwork represent your individual point of view, imagination, creativity, and individuality?
2. Explain your purpose in developing this artwork and describe how you achieved that goal. Describe any influences from art history, artists, or art periods or styles.
3. What elements or principles of art did you use, and how did you select and organize the visual aspects of this work?
