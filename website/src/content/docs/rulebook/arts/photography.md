Not only do you carry around your camera with you all the time, but you see everything as an opportunity for the perfect photo. Those other artists, yeah, they're cool - but you prefer a lens and the dark room for your creative pursuits.

**Format:** In-Person  
**Early Submission:** None

## On-Site Submission Requirements
- Photograph submission
- Written artist statement (3 hard copies, 500 words maximum)

## Registration Guidelines

1. No more than **four (4) students** from each school can register for this competition.
2. This competition is **NOT** a team effort. Each competitor must turn in a separate piece of work.
3. MIST ID and order of competition will be pre-assigned on a random selection basis.

## General Art Rules

1. All artwork must be labeled with **MIST ID ONLY**. Competitor name must not appear anywhere.
2. No more than **$100** can be spent on supplies.
3. Artwork must relate to the overall MIST Theme.
4. Any submissions including **AI-generated work** (whether used for the art or written statements) is subject to disqualification.
5. For scoring, students who do not receive an interview can only receive a **maximum of 80 points**.

## Procedures

1. Photographs should **avoid realistic, representational rendering of lifelike human or animal form**. If in doubt, contact your Regional Competitions Headquarters.

2. Students are **NOT** permitted to utilize AI Generators for editing or generation.

3. A typewritten statement (500 words max.) must be included explaining what the photograph is about and what inspired you.

4. Each participant will be interviewed for **five (5) minutes** to describe the purpose and inspiration of the photograph.

5. If there are more than **15 entries**, only the top 15 entries (based on photograph and written statement) will be interviewed.

## Checklist
- [ ] Photography submission
- [ ] Written statement (500 words max)
- [ ] Both submission and written statement labeled with MIST ID and competition name

## Interview Questions

Judges will ask:
1. How did you aim to convey this year's theme in your work? In what ways does your photograph represent your individual point of view, imagination, creativity, and individuality?
2. Explain your purpose in developing this photograph and describe how you achieved that goal. Describe any influences from photography history, photographers, or styles that contributed to the development of this work.
3. What elements or principles of photography/art did you use, and how did you select and organize the visual aspects of this work?
