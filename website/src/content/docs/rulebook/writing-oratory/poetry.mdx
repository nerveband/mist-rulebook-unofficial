Competitors must submit an original piece of poetry never before published within a 40-line limit. The piece must clearly relate back to the theme but also be applicable to a general audience.

## Format
Virtual (Online)

## Early Submission Requirements

- Competitors must submit their Poetry piece **2 weeks before** the Regional Tournament via MyMIST.

## Registration Guidelines

1. A limit of **two (2) students** from each team may register for this competition.
2. Each student competes **individually** — collaboration is not permitted.

## Procedure

<Steps>

1. Poetry must clearly reflect upon any aspect of this year's **theme**.

2. Submissions must be labeled with **MIST ID and title only**.
   - Competitor's name must not appear anywhere on the work.

3. Competitors may incorporate any style or genre: rhymed, lyric, dramatic, narrative, or free verse.

4. **No AI permitted** — usage is grounds for point loss and/or disqualification.

5. All poems must have a **title** and not exceed **40 lines** (excluding title).

6. Poems must be the **original work** of the entrant.
   - Entries must not have been published, self-published, or broadcast before January 2026.

7. A **PDF copy** must be submitted via MyMIST 2 weeks before the Regional Tournament.
   - Late submissions result in point deduction.

8. This is an **entirely virtual competition** — no copy required at tournament.

9. Entries should be typed in **black ink only**.

10. Entries can be double or single-spaced.

11. Poetry submissions **must include** a filled-out "Poetry Analyzing Written Statement."

</Steps>

## Checklist

- [ ] PDF copy of Poetry Piece on MyMIST (2 weeks prior)
- [ ] Written Poetry Analyzing Statement
- [ ] Labeled with MIST ID and competition name

## Poetry Analyzing Written Statement

*Responses to each section must be typed, not handwritten.*

### Section 1: Poem Identity
- Form of Poetry (e.g., Ballad, Sonnet, etc.)

### Section 2: Explanation of Poem
- Provide an explanation in **250 words or less**

### Section 3: Elements of Poetry
- Identify at least **five (5) elements** of poetry from your writing
- State line number, type of element, and explanation
- Examples: alliteration, metaphor, assonance, personification

### Section 4: Example of Inferences
- Provide an example of inference from your writing
- State line number and explain what you wished to infer
- Examples: historical inference, literary inference

### Section 5: Mechanics, Grammar, Spelling
*Answer all within 500 words or less:*

1. In what ways does your piece represent your individuality? What inspired or influenced you?
2. Are there subtleties you hoped would come across without explanation?
3. Do you feel you conveyed the theme well? How did you do so?
