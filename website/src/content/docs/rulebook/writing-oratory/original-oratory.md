Competitors present a memorized original oration within a seven (7) minute period. The oration must relate back to the theme but also be applicable to a general audience.

**Format:** In-Person  
**Early Submission:** None

:::note[Changed in 2026]
Memorization is now an officially scored component within the rubric.
:::

## On-Site Requirements
- Three (3) copies of the oratory piece must be submitted to judges on-site at competition time.

## Registration Guidelines

1. A limit of **two (2) students** from each team may register.
2. Each student competes individually; collaboration not permitted.

## Procedures

1. MIST ID and order of competition will be pre-assigned randomly.

2. All competitors report to the competition room at the designated time.

3. The Orator must provide **3 hardcopies** of the script at competition orientation.
   - MIST will not photocopy any scripts.
   - Compete with the script submitted at that time.

4. Orations must be in **English**.

5. The piece must clearly relate to the theme but be applicable to a general audience.

6. Orations must be labeled with **MIST ID ONLY**.

7. The orator must be **truthful**. Any non-factual reference, especially personal, must be identified.

8. The oration may not contain more than **150 words of quoted material**.
   - Extensive paraphrasing from other sources is prohibited.
   - Plagiarizing or extensive paraphrasing = disqualification.
   - Quoted material in another language must be translated (not counted in 150-word limit).

9. **No visual aids** may be used.

10. An orator holding a manuscript or notes will be ranked last.

11. **Time limit: 7 minutes maximum** with 30-second grace period.
    - Violation results in being ranked last.
    - Judges must give time signals per competitor's request.

12. **Electronic devices not permitted.**

## Scoring Rubric

| Section | Category | Points |
|---------|----------|--------|
| A | Technical | /10 |
| B | Introduction | /10 |
| B | Body | /15 |
| B | Conclusion | /10 |
| C | Analysis and Content | /15 |
| C | Delivery | /15 |
| C | Language | /15 |
| D | Overall Experience | /10 |
| **Total** | | **/100** |

### Section A: Technical (10 points)
- On time for competition
- Speech finished within 7 minutes
- No identifiers aside from MIST ID
- Looks credible and dressed for interview

### Section B: Content

**Introduction (10 points)**
- Got audience's attention
- Topic clearly stated/addressed
- Previewed key ideas

**Body (15 points)**
- Adequate time devoted to each division
- Logical progression of ideas

**Conclusion (10 points)**
- Tied speech together
- Answer clear
- Note of finality

### Section C: Presentation

**Analysis and Content (15 points)**
- Oration revolves around theme
- Informative content
- Sufficient logic, facts, examples, expert opinion
- Information pertinent to topic

**Delivery (15 points)**
- Natural, lively, spontaneous
- Clear enunciation, appropriate volume
- Variety in rate, pause, pitch
- Poise and confidence
- Varied gestures, motivated movement, direct eye contact
- **Speech memorized**

**Language (15 points)**
- Command of conversational style
- Language precise, grammatically correct, vivid

### Section D: Overall Experience (1-10 points)
Rate the entire experience:
- 1 = very poor
- 4-6 = average
- 10 = exceptional
