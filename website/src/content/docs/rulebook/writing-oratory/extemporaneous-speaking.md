The art of extemporaneous speaking is for those who can always make conversation. Delivering a coherent, engaging, and moving speech is easy enough, right? Now try doing that on the spot with only 30 minutes of preparation!

**Format:** In-Person

:::note[Changed in 2026]
Preparation time has been adjusted to 30 minutes to support stronger research and performance readiness.
:::

## Registration Guidelines

1. A limit of **two (2) students** from each school may register for this competition.
2. Each student competes individually, so collaboration is not permitted.

## Procedures

1. All competitors report to the competition room at the same time.

2. Paper and pen/pencil will be provided, but students may bring their own.

3. Students will **choose one topic from a selection of three** at the start of their turn.
   - Topics will be relevant to the annual theme or current events.
   - Topics are phrased as questions.
   - Example: "Does a significant rift exist between African-American Muslims and immigrant Muslim communities?"

4. Depending on sign-ups, the competition might be conducted in a series of rounds.

5. Students may bring **printed reference materials** (newspapers, articles) or quotations, but prepared notes are **not allowed**.
   - Materials must be approved by the Moderator.
   - If references are used, you **MUST cite** them during your speech.
   - Annotations not permitted.

6. **Thirty (30) minutes** before the contest begins, the first speaker shall draw three topics, choose one, and return the others. Other competitors draw at seven-minute intervals.

7. After choosing a topic, withdraw to the preparation area and have **30 minutes** to plan without conferring with anyone.
   - No prepared notes or outlines in the preparation room.
   - Reference to books, magazines, and bibliographical files is permitted.

8. Once chosen, you may neither change nor alter your topic.

9. **No visual aids** may be used in delivery.

10. A note card may be used in preliminary rounds but **not in elimination rounds**.

11. **Time limit: 7 minutes maximum** with 30-second grace period.
    - Violation results in being ranked last when visible time signals have been given.
    - Judges must give time signals per competitor's request.

12. **Electronic devices not permitted.**

## Scoring Rubric

| Section | Category | Points |
|---------|----------|--------|
| A | Technical | /10 |
| B | Introduction | /10 |
| B | Body | /15 |
| B | Conclusion | /10 |
| C | Analysis and Content | /15 |
| C | Delivery | /15 |
| C | Language | /15 |
| D | Overall Experience | /10 |
| **Total** | | **/100** |

:::note[References]
Students do NOT have to use references. If they do, they may be awarded 2 points. However, if a student uses references and does NOT cite them in speech, 3 points will be deducted.
:::

### Section A: Technical (10 points)
- On time for competition
- Speech finished within 7 minutes (or before grace period ended)
- No identifiers aside from MIST ID
- Looks credible and dressed for interview

### Section B: Content

**Introduction (10 points)**
- Got audience's attention
- Topic clearly stated/addressed
- Previewed key ideas

**Body (15 points)**
- Adequate time devoted to each division
- Logical progression of ideas

**Conclusion (10 points)**
- Tied speech together
- Answer to question was clear
- Note of finality

### Section C: Presentation

**Analysis and Content (15 points)**
- Answered the question
- Informative content
- Sufficient use of logic, facts, examples, expert opinion
- Information pertinent to topic

**Delivery (15 points)**
- Natural, lively, spontaneous delivery
- Clear enunciation, appropriate volume
- Variety in rate, pause, pitch
- Poise and confidence
- Varied gestures, motivated movement, direct eye contact

**Language (15 points)**
- Command of conversational style
- Language precise, grammatically correct, vivid

### Section D: Overall Experience (1-10 points)
Rate the entire experience:
- 1 = very poor
- 4-6 = average
- 10 = exceptional
