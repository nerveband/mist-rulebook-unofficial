Competitors are allotted 60 minutes to compose a handwritten essay on a randomly selected topic related to the theme.

## Registration Guidelines

1. A limit of **two (2) students** from each team may register for this competition.
2. Each student competes individually, so collaboration is not permitted.

## Procedures

1. **ONLY MIST ID** should be written on the paper. No other personal identifiers should be used (name, spirit team, school, etc.)

2. All competitors are to report to the competition room at the same time, designated in the competition schedule.

3. Paper and pen/pencil will be provided, but students may bring their own.

4. Students will **choose one topic from a selection of three**.
   - Topics will be relevant to the annual theme or current events.

5. After topics are chosen and written on the top of the submission page, students are allotted **60 minutes** to complete their essay in English.

6. Students are allowed to bring **printed reference materials** (newspapers, articles) or quotations relevant to the theme or current affairs, but prepared notes or personal essays are **not allowed**.
   - Materials must be reviewed and approved by the Competition Moderator before the competition begins.
   - Annotations within materials will **not** be permitted (highlighting, underlining, post-its, margin notes).

7. **Electronic device usage is not permitted** during the competition.

## Scoring Rubric

| Section | Category | Points |
|---------|----------|--------|
| A | Technical | /5 |
| B | Conventions | /10 |
| B | Ideas and Development | /20 |
| B | Organization | /15 |
| B | Sentence Fluency | /15 |
| C | Word Choice | /20 |
| C | Voice | /15 |
| **Total** | | **/100** |

### Section A: Technical (5 points)
- Participant is on time
- Stopped writing when time was called
- No identifiers aside from MIST ID

### Section B: Content

**Conventions (10 points)**
- Spelling mistakes: -1 for each error
- Punctuation mistakes: -1 for each error
- Grammatical mistakes: -2 for each error
- Correct citations: +1 for each
- Missing citations: -2 for each error

**Ideas and Development (20 points)**
- Answered one of the topics with sufficient detail
- Unique approach to addressing the topic
- Stayed on topic throughout
- Main idea clear to reader

**Organization (15 points)**
- Introduction grabs attention with unique start, clear thesis, concise supporting points
- Adequate details, examples, and support for each point
- Well-developed paragraphs with own main ideas
- Conclusion links back to introduction

**Sentence Fluency (15 points)**
- Varied sentence types (simple and complex)
- Most sentences begin with different words
- Sentences flow well together when read aloud
- Logical progression of ideas

### Section C: Style

**Word Choice (20 points)**
- Thoughtful adjectives create vivid imagery
- Strong action verbs ("cruise" instead of "drive")
- Precise nouns ("7th Grade students" instead of "students")
- Words create color and texture; writer took risks

**Voice (15 points)**
- Writer shows passion about topic
- Tone is natural and consistent
- Writer captures mood with words
