Think you have what it takes for the Olympics? Earn the title of true Math Olympian in this intense battle of numbers.

**Format:** In-Person (Bracket Competition)

:::note[Changed in 2026]
The competition now includes expanded content coverage along with supplemented practice questions to better guide student preparation.
:::

## Registration Guidelines

1. A maximum of **four (4) students** from each team may register.
2. Students compete **individually**.

## Competition Format

Math Olympics is a **Bracket Competition** with elimination rounds.

### Rounds
- Preliminary Rounds
- Quarterfinals
- Semifinals
- Finals

## Content Areas

The competition covers:
- Algebra
- Geometry
- Trigonometry
- Number Theory
- Probability & Statistics
- Calculus concepts (basic)

## Procedures

1. All competitors report to the competition room at the designated time.

2. **No calculators** are permitted unless specified by your Regional HQ.

3. **No electronic devices** during competition.

4. Paper and pencils will be provided.

5. Each round consists of problems to be solved within a time limit.

6. Answers must be clearly written and legible.

7. Partial credit may be awarded for work shown.

## Problem Types

- Short answer
- Multiple choice
- Show-your-work problems

## Scoring

- Points awarded for correct answers
- Tiebreakers determined by:
  1. Time to completion
  2. Number of problems attempted
  3. Difficulty of problems solved

## Preparation Tips

1. Review algebra, geometry, and trigonometry fundamentals
2. Practice mental math and estimation
3. Work on problem-solving speed
4. Study number theory concepts
5. Familiarize yourself with common competition problem types

## Practice Resources

Practice questions and study materials are available on the MIST National website: [getmistified.com/rulebook](https://www.getmistified.com/rulebook)
