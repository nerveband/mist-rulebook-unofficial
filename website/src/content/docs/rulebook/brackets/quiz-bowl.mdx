Do you get an adrenaline rush at knowing the answer to any question asked? Are you a memorization whiz? Then MIST Quiz Bowl is the competition for you!

## Format
In-Person

## Registration Guidelines

1. Teams consist of a minimum of **two (2)** members and a maximum of **six (6)**.
2. At any given time, only a maximum of **four (4)** students can play.
3. A student can substitute for another student **ONLY between rounds**.
4. If your team has six (6) students, there will be four (4) starters and two (2) on the bench.
5. A team of four (4) must have all students starting.

<Aside type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
The more students on your bowl team, the better! Each student can specialize in one of the five categories.
</Aside>

## Tournament Structure

Each tournament has:
- Three (3) Preliminary Rounds
- One (1) Quarterfinal
- One (1) Semifinal
- One (1) Final Round

**Elimination** for preliminary rounds only takes place after all three rounds are completed.

### Room Requirements
Every MIST Bowl room requires a minimum of 3 teams and maximum of 4.

## Preliminary/Quarterfinal/Semifinal Procedure

A competition consists of **three rounds** with a total of **38 questions**.

- Each round has 10-14 questions from 1-2 categories
- All 5 categories are covered by end of competition
- Questions are printed in one packet with appropriate point values pre-assigned

<Steps>

1. Each team is allowed 6 members, with no more than 4 participating per round.

2. Teams may shuffle players **ONLY between rounds**.

3. Judges announce which two categories are being played in each round so teams may arrange accordingly.

4. If questions are challenged, judges deliberate after the round before awarding or deducting points.

5. Study materials are **NOT** permitted at any point during competition (including for non-competing team members).

</Steps>

## Round 1: Current Events

Round 1 **ALWAYS** covers only Current Events.

### Format
- Series of **10 toss-up questions** consisting of two parts each
- Moderators will prompt "for 50 points" at the half-point mark

### Scoring

| Scenario | Points |
|----------|--------|
| First part correct | +100 |
| First part incorrect | -100 |
| Second part correct (after "for 50 points") | +50 |
| Second part incorrect | -50 |

### Rules

<Steps>

1. Once buzzed in, judges must stop reading and are **not** allowed to repeat the question.

2. If answered incorrectly, the next team to buzz gets the question for **full original point value**.

3. Teams have **10 seconds** to answer after buzzing.

4. There is **no "Done" rule** for this round.

</Steps>

### Example Question
> "This tournament's mascot, Mola the lion, appeared throughout venues in cities such as Bafoussam, Garoua, and Limbe. Despite multiple postponements due to infrastructure delays and COVID-19 concerns, this competition proceeded under strict health protocols. **For 50 points:** name this international football competition, held from January 9 to February 6, 2021, in Cameroon, which Senegal won for the first time."
>
> **Answer:** the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations (accept AFCON 2021)

## Round 2: Topic-Based

### Format
- Two categories announced at the beginning
- Questions in **ascending point value**: 20, 40, 60, 80, 100, 120, 140
- First 7 questions from Category 1, next 7 from Category 2
- Categories are **randomized** in each game

### Rules

<Steps>

1. Judges read the point value first, then the question.

2. Competitors may **buzz early** if they can anticipate the answer.

3. If buzzing early, the student must **immediately** give the answer.

4. **10 seconds** to answer after buzzing, 10 seconds to finish = 20 seconds total.

5. If incorrect, floor opens to all other teams.

6. Once buzzed in, judges **stop reading** and cannot repeat the question.

7. Other teams can buzz as soon as judge says "incorrect" or wait for the re-read.

</Steps>

### Scoring

| Scenario | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Correct answer | Full point value |
| Incorrect answer | -10% of point value |
| Steal (correct) | Half point value |
| Steal (incorrect) | -Half point value |

<Aside type="caution" title="Important">
Questions are considered **dead after 2 incorrect answers**. Only full points and half points are possible for the first 2 teams.
</Aside>

## Round 3: Topic-Based (Same as Round 2)

- Follows same format as Round 2
- Two different categories announced
- Penalties apply for incorrect answers
- Questions dead after 2 incorrect answers

## Finals Procedure

Finals consist of **three rounds** with a total of **40 questions**.

### Team Rules
- 6 members maximum, 4 participating per round
- Players may shuffle **ONLY between rounds**

### Finals Round 1

<Steps>

1. Free-form questions read aloud by judge.

2. Teams may buzz before entire question is read.

3. **10 seconds** to answer after buzz, 10 seconds to complete = 20 seconds total.

4. Correct answer wins points. Incorrect answer **does NOT penalize**.

5. If incorrect, floor reopens after judge says "incorrect."

6. Second team to answer gets **half points**.

7. Only **two teams** can attempt any single question.

8. Each question worth **100 points**.

</Steps>

### Finals Round 2

Same rules as Finals Round 1, **except**:
- All questions worth **200 points**
- **Penalties apply** for incorrect answers

### Finals Round 3: Jeopardy Style

<Steps>

1. Team with **lowest score** chooses category and point value first.
   - Example: "History for 300 please."

2. After question is read, any team may buzz.

3. Correct answer = team selects another question.

4. Incorrect answer = penalty (same as Round 2).

5. Floor opens immediately after judge says "incorrect."

6. If no team answers in 10 seconds, same team chooses again.

7. Questions dead after 2 incorrect answers.

</Steps>

<Aside type="note" title="Note">
A Jeopardy matrix showing category-value grid will be used. An assistant crosses off each box when that question is asked. Scores are not released to competitors after rounds.
</Aside>
