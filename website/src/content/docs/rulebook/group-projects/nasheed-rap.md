Perform an original nasheed or rap that reflects Islamic values and connects to the annual theme.

**Format:** In-Person  
**Gender Segregated:** Yes

## Registration Guidelines

1. **Team of 3-6 students** from each school.
2. This is a **team competition**.
3. Competition is **gender segregated**:
   - Brothers Nasheed/Rap
   - Sisters Nasheed/Rap

## Requirements

1. Performance must be **original work** created by the team.

2. Lyrics must reflect **Islamic values** and connect to the annual theme.

3. **No musical instruments** are permitted.
   - Vocals and beatboxing only
   - Pre-recorded backing tracks may be used (check with Regional HQ)

4. Performance time: Check with your Regional HQ for time limits.

5. All team members must **actively participate** in the performance.

6. **Lyrics must be submitted** to judges before performance.
   - Include MIST ID only (no names)

## Content Guidelines

- Must be appropriate and respectful
- No inappropriate language or themes
- Should inspire and uplift the audience
- Must relate to the annual MIST theme

## Performance Elements

Teams will be judged on:

1. **Lyrics/Writing**
   - Originality
   - Theme relevance
   - Message clarity
   - Poetic elements

2. **Vocal Performance**
   - Clarity and enunciation
   - Pitch and tone
   - Harmonies (if applicable)

3. **Stage Presence**
   - Energy and engagement
   - Movement and choreography
   - Audience connection

4. **Teamwork**
   - Coordination
   - Equal participation
   - Chemistry

5. **Overall Impact**
   - Entertainment value
   - Memorability
   - Inspiration factor

## Spectator Guidelines

Students who spectate must abide by **gender segregation guidelines**:
- Brothers are not permitted to spectate Sisters Nasheed/Rap
- Sisters are not permitted to spectate Brothers Nasheed/Rap

## Checklist
- [ ] Original lyrics written
- [ ] Lyrics submitted to judges (with MIST ID)
- [ ] Performance rehearsed
- [ ] Backing track prepared (if using)
- [ ] All team members ready to perform
